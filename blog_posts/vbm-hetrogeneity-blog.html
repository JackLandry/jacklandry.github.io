Varying Effects of Universal Vote By Mail?

      To assess how the effects of Universal VBM varies by preexisting mail voting rates, I test whether the effects of Universal VBM differ between the counties below the median pre adoption rate compared to those above the median.1 I re-estimate the same baseline model used by Thompson et al. with county and election-year fixed effects. However, I interact the treatment variable with an indicator if a treated state had below the median share of mail voters pre-treatment.

      Overall, I find weak evidence that universal vote by mail differs much between counties that had lower levels of vote by mail before making it universal compared to those with higher levels. Any partisan effects are even smaller in low pre-implementation VBM counties, and turnout might be slightly higher. All things equal, this should add to the evidence that universal VBM will not cause dramatic changes in turnout or composition of the electorate.
